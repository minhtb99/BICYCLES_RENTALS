Celebrate National Bike Month Exploring Santa Monica

  • May 10, 2022
  • 1:18 pm

Did you know that May is National Bike Month? Whether you’re an experienced rider or haven’t hopped on a bicycle in years, it’s the perfect time to enjoy the many benefits of bike riding.

With over 100 miles of bikeways, breathtaking views, and ocean air, there’s no better place to celebrate this annual event than beautiful Santa Monica! Getting out on your bike to soak up some sunshine is the perfect way to explore all that Santa Monica has to offer.

If you don’t have your own bicycle, it doesn’t mean that you’ll be left out of the celebration! There are plenty of coastal bike rentals, like Queens Bicycles Rentals in the heart of Santa Monica’s most famous sights and bike paths.

Here are some of the spots you don’t want to miss when you head out to explore Santa Monica’s unique and fun-filled neighborhoods, sights, and attractions.

City Exploring

Santa Monica has all the charm you’d expect from a modern beachfront community, making riding through town an enjoyable way to explore. In fact, there are four distinctive neighborhoods in Santa Monica, each filled with its own adventures and offerings.

Downtown Santa Monica

You don’t want to miss the downtown area and Main Street for shopping, dining, and sightseeing. Two of Santa Monica’s largest shopping destinations are located in the downtown area, making it a great bike ride destination.

Downtown, you can visit Santa Monica Place and the Third Street Promenade. Plus, if you visit on Saturday, Sunday, or Wednesday, you can check out the locally famous farmer’s market!

San Vicente Boulevard

Another perfect destination for your National Bike Month adventure is San Vicente Blvd. It’s a relaxed three-mile route that will take you through residential neighborhood areas and popular businesses. It’s the perfect ride to catch some wind in your face underneath some of California’s tree-lined roadways.

Pico Boulevard

For something a little more trendy and artsy, visit the Pico Boulevard neighborhood. There, you’ll find art galleries, music venues, and loads of murals to enjoy. There’s also a planetarium and performing arts center around Santa Monica College.

Beach Exploring

Your day of exploration wouldn’t be complete without a ride to the beach. Santa Monica is known for its miles of bike paths, including some of the most famous ones that you can hop onto right from the beach area.

The Marvin Braude Bike Trail (“The Strand”)

The Marvin Braude Bike Trail (also known as “The Strand”) is a 22-mile paved bike path that will take you on an adventure through some of the most iconic sights and attractions in Santa Monica. You’ll ride underneath the Santa Monica Pier, past world-famous Muscle Beach, and through some of the most popular and beautiful beaches on the California coastline.

Santa Monica Pier

For something less strenuous, you can ride over to the Santa Monica Pier neighborhood located along Ocean Avenue. There, you can lock up your ride and check out the restaurants and other attractions in the area before you head back at your leisure.

Celebrate National Bike Month Exploring On Your Bike Rental

If you’ve been meaning to get out and explore all that Santa Monica has to offer, National Bike Month is your reason to do it! A bike rental is one of the easiest and most fun ways to explore Santa Monica and check out the different sights and attractions.

Whether you ride The Strand, check out The Pier, or downtown, you’re sure to have a great time on your bike riding adventure.

When your day is ending and it’s time to return your bike to Queens Bicycles Rentals, you should check out Big Dean’s Ocean Front Café for a bite to eat or a refreshing drink. It’s the perfect end to a perfect day exploring Santa Monica.
